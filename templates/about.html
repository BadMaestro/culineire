Not a recipe museum

CulinEire exists to keep Irish cooking useful, findable and alive.

A recipe should not disappear after one scroll. A family dish should not sit behind glass. A good kitchen note should be easy to save, share and return to before cooking.

Working Kitchen

Memory, ingredients, people, new dishes and a place for chefs, home cooks and recipe writers.

The guiding thought

Keep the flavour of the past and let it work in today's kitchen.

Why CulinEire Exists

Good kitchen ideas disappear too quickly.

A recipe is opened, cooked once and forgotten. A useful idea appears in a feed and is gone. A chef makes a small discovery, then loses the note before the next service or family dinner.

CulinEire is designed as a working kitchen shelf: a calm place to collect recipes, sources, short ideas and people worth returning to.

Irish Foundation

Heritage, Without The Glass Case

Irish cooking gives CulinEire its foundation: home food, baking, stews, fish, meat, dairy, seasonal ingredients and local produce.

The past matters here, but the kitchen is not locked in the past.

Sources, stories and context

Respect, not nostalgia

Quality And Partners

Useful partnership, not advertising noise.

CulinEire can work with partners connected to food, craft, training, ingredients, equipment, markets, cafes and the restaurant trade. But a sponsor must fit the site, the culture and the honest tone.

  • Sponsored material should be clearly labelled.
  • Partners can support a theme or prize, but cannot choose a winner.
  • Quality, fairness and usefulness matter more than short-term money.
