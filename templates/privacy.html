Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Bearcave Limited ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects personal data in connection with the CulinEire website at culineire.ie. It applies to all visitors, registered users, and authors.

This policy is written in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and the Data Protection Acts 1988-2018 (Ireland).

1

Data Controller

The data controller for personal data processed through CulinEire is:

  • Bearcave Limited
  • Company No. 658124
  • 2 The Fairways, Tir Cluain, Midleton, Co. Cork, P25 W8W3, Ireland
  • Email: culineire@bearcave.ie
2

What Personal Data We Collect

We may collect the following personal data:

  • Account data: username, email address, display name, and selected default avatar when you register.
  • Author profile data: biographical information, recipes, articles, and other content you voluntarily publish on CulinEire.
  • Messages and contact submissions: the content of messages you send through our contact or messaging tools, including your name and email address.
  • Content reports: name, email address, and the details you provide when submitting a content report.
  • Moderation records: records of moderation actions taken on your account or content, and any communications relating to those actions.
  • Security and audit logs: login events, IP addresses, and other technical information collected for security and abuse prevention purposes.
  • Technical data: browser type, device type, and approximate location inferred from IP address, collected when you use the site.

Visitors who do not register an account are not asked to provide personal data. We do not collect data through advertising networks, behavioural tracking, or profiling cookies.

3

How We Use Your Personal Data

  • To operate and manage your account and author profile.
  • To publish and moderate the content you submit.
  • To send transactional emails, such as account notifications and moderation outcomes.
  • To respond to enquiries and content reports.
  • To protect the site from spam, abuse, and unauthorised access.
  • To comply with legal obligations, including data subject requests and communications with regulatory authorities.
4

Legal Bases for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases under GDPR Article 6:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to provide the CulinEire service to registered users and authors, including account management and content publishing.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): Security monitoring, fraud prevention, site administration, and maintaining audit logs, where our interests do not override your rights.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): Where we are required to process or retain data to comply with Irish or EU law.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): Where we ask for and receive your explicit consent, for example when you agree to specific communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.
5

Cookies and Tracking

CulinEire uses only essential, functional cookies. We do not use advertising, analytics, or profiling cookies. For full details, see our Cookie Policy.

6

Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

We may share data with the following categories of recipients:

  • Hosting provider: CulinEire is hosted on Linode (Akamai) infrastructure. Your data is stored on servers in the EU where practicable. Akamai acts as a data processor under a data processing agreement.
  • Email delivery: Transactional emails are sent via a third-party mail provider. Only the data necessary to deliver the email is shared.
  • Cloudflare: CulinEire uses Cloudflare for DNS resolution and the Turnstile CAPTCHA service. Cloudflare may process limited technical information such as IP addresses. See Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.
  • Regulatory authorities: We may disclose personal data to the Data Protection Commission, An Garda Siochana, or other lawful authorities where required by law.
7

Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your account and for a reasonable period after deletion to enable account recovery and resolve disputes.
  • Published content: Retained for as long as it is published on CulinEire. Content you request to remove will be deleted within a reasonable period.
  • Security logs: Retained for up to 12 months for abuse prevention and security investigation purposes.
  • Legal and compliance records: Retained for the period required by applicable Irish law, which may be up to 7 years.
8

Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access (Article 15): You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16): You may request correction of inaccurate personal data.
  • Right to erasure (Article 17): You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, or there is no other legal basis for processing. Some data may be retained where there is a legal obligation to do so.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18): You may request that we restrict how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Article 20): Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, you may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Article 21): You may object to processing based on our legitimate interests. We will comply unless we have compelling legitimate grounds that override your rights.
  • Rights related to automated decision-making (Article 22): CulinEire does not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces significant effects on you.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at culineire@bearcave.ie. We will respond within one month. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request.

9

Right to Complain

If you believe that your personal data has been processed in breach of GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Commission (DPC) of Ireland:

  • Website: www.dataprotection.ie
  • Address: Data Protection Commission, 21 Fitzwilliam Square South, Dublin 2, D02 RD28, Ireland
  • Phone: +353 (0)761 104 800
10

Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include encrypted connections (HTTPS), access controls, and regular security reviews.

CulinEire is hosted on third-party infrastructure. While we maintain reasonable precautions and backups, we cannot guarantee complete protection against all risks including hardware failure or cyberattack. In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Data Protection Commission within 72 hours and will notify affected individuals where required by law.

11

Children's Privacy

CulinEire is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16 without verifiable parental consent. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us without appropriate consent, please contact us at culineire@bearcave.ie and we will take steps to remove it promptly.

12

International Transfers

We aim to store and process personal data within the European Economic Area (EEA). Where data is transferred outside the EEA (for example, via third-party service providers), we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

13

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify registered users of material changes where practicable. The effective date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was last revised. Continued use of CulinEire after changes are published constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

14

Contact Us

For privacy enquiries, data subject requests, or to report a concern about how your data is handled, please contact us at culineire@bearcave.ie or write to Bearcave Limited, 2 The Fairways, Tir Cluain, Midleton, Co. Cork, P25 W8W3, Ireland.
