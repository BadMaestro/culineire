How Chef Battles Works

The Chef Battles Arena is a live cooking competition platform where Chefs challenge each other, cook under combat conditions, and let the audience decide the winner. These rules govern all battles, tokens, artifacts and audience participation on CulinEire.

Rules apply to all battles. Tokens and artifacts are governed by the Purchases & VAT policy.

Eligibility

  • You must be 18 years of age or older to participate in Chef Battles, send appreciation gifts, or purchase tokens.
  • By creating a Chef Battle profile or making any purchase in the arena, you confirm that you are at least 18 years old.
  • CulinEire reserves the right to request age verification at any time. Accounts where verification fails will be suspended.
  • Battle profiles and token balances are personal and non-transferable. Account sharing or transfer is not permitted.
  • You must have a valid CulinEire account in good standing to enter the arena.

The Challenge

  • Any Chef with an active profile can challenge another chef.
  • Challenges can only be sent between Chefs of the same or an adjacent rank. The CulinEire Hero is exempt from this restriction.
  • The challenged Chef has 48 hours to accept or refuse.
  • Refusing a challenge costs 15 Battle Moves. Repeated refusals are recorded on the public profile.
  • Challenges are always linked to a specific recipe. The challenger can either select an existing approved recipe as the battle theme, or propose a new theme — in which case both Chefs submit new recipes within 48 hours.
  • An accepted challenge opens a Battle Room with a shared theme set by the challenger.

The Changing Room

  • Once a challenge is accepted, both Chefs enter the Changing Room — a private preparation stage before combat begins.
  • In the Changing Room, each Chef reviews the battle theme and prepares their ingredient list based on their chosen recipe.
  • The Changing Room is private: neither Chef can see the opponent’s ingredient selection until combat begins.
  • Chefs must select their locked ingredients (2 per Chef) and finalise their combat setup before entering the Arena.
  • The Changing Room stage is time-limited. If a Chef does not complete their setup within the allowed time, they may forfeit their combat advantage.

The Battle

  • Each Chef has 48 hours to submit a recipe as their battle entry.
  • Entries must be original recipes created specifically for the battle theme.
  • If the Challenger does not submit within 48 hours and the Opponent has submitted, the Challenger loses automatically.
  • If the Opponent does not submit and the Challenger has submitted, the Opponent loses by default and the Challenger wins.
  • If neither Chef submits within 48 hours, the battle is cancelled. No rating points are awarded and no Crown is granted.
  • Technical victories (wins by default due to no-show) do not award the Crown. The Crown is reserved for battles where both Chefs complete the full flow: submission, combat, cooking and voting.
  • Only one entry per Chef per battle is allowed.
  • After both entries are submitted, the Combat Phase begins.

Combat Phase

  • Each Chef selects a menu of ingredients from their recipe. Both menus are equal in size.
  • Each Chef locks 2 ingredients to protect them from attack.
  • Chefs take turns attacking. Each attack or defence move costs between 1 and 5 Battle Moves. Additional power can be added through combat artifacts.
  • A hit lands on any unlocked ingredient in the opponent's menu. A hit ingredient is marked as defeated and must be replaced or removed from the final recipe.
  • The Combat Phase uses a 3-point system. The first Chef to land 3 successful hits wins Round 1 (Combat).
  • Audience likes count as support. A Chef who receives audience likes earns 1 Battle Move per like, at any time during an active battle.

Biathlon

  • The Chef who won Round 1 of Combat fires up to 3 blind shots at the opponent's ingredient list.
  • A shot that lands on a locked ingredient bounces back and counts as a wasted shot.
  • Results: 1 to 3 successful hits depending on aim and opponent's locks.
  • Killed ingredients must be swapped or removed before cooking begins.

Cooking Phase

  • After combat, both Chefs cook their final recipes using the surviving ingredient list.
  • Each Chef photographs the finished dish and uploads it to the battle room.
  • A CulinEire moderator reviews the photo to confirm the dish is real, follows the rules, and image rights are clear.
  • Once both entries are approved, the battle moves to public voting.

Voting

  • The public votes for their favourite dish. Voting is open for 2 days.
  • Each visitor gets one vote per battle. No account required to vote.
  • The Chef with the most votes wins the battle and receives the Crown.
  • In the event of a tie, the battle is recorded as a draw and no crown is awarded.
  • The Crown is awarded only after a full completed battle (both Chefs submitted, combat completed, cooking phase completed and public voting finished).
  • If a Chef already holds the Crown when entering a battle, the Crown status remains active regardless of the battle outcome. A technical win (no-show) does not transfer the Crown from one Chef to another.
  • The Crown is held for 24 hours and is visible on the winner's public profile.

The Ceremony

  • After voting closes, the Ceremony is the public resolution stage. The battle result, vote totals and winner are announced in the Battle Room.
  • The winner receives: a rating increase (+25 points), a Crown valid for 24 hours (full battles only), and a guaranteed artifact drop.
  • The defeated Chef receives: a rating adjustment (−15 points) and a 50% chance of a consolation artifact drop.
  • Both Chefs receive a public Battle Record entry on their profile showing their result, opponent, theme and vote totals.
  • If the winner already held the Crown, the 24-hour Crown timer resets from the moment the Ceremony is completed.
  • All Ceremony events (result, Crown award, rating changes, artifact drops) are visible in the site-wide activity feed and both Chefs’ profiles.

Rating & Ranks

  • Every new Chef starts with 0 rating points at Kitchen Porter rank. Winning a completed battle adds 25 rating points; losing deducts 15, with rating never falling below 0.
  • The rating ladder is: Kitchen Porter (0–99), Prep Chef (100–199), Commis Chef (200–299), Chef de Partie (300–399), Sous Chef (400–499), Head Chef (500–599), Executive Chef (600–699), and Culinary Master (700+).
  • Culinary Master is the highest rank available through competition. CulinEire Hero is a unique site-owner status and cannot be earned through rating, wins or battles.
  • The Rankings are ordered primarily by rating. Wins are used as a later tie-breaker.
  • Current and best win streaks track consecutive victories on the Chef profile. A win streak is a performance record, not an additional rating bonus.

Battle Moves

  • Battle Moves are earned through activity: +5 per approved recipe, +5 per approved article, +1 per approved Pinch, +1 per unique audience like received, +10 for winning a battle, +1 for participating in a battle.
  • A minimum of 10 Battle Moves is required to issue a challenge.
  • When enrolling as a Chef, all previously earned moves from recipes, articles, Pinches and likes are calculated and credited. Up to 60 moves go directly to your battle balance; any remainder is stored in your chest.

Tokens

  • Tokens (T) are the arena currency. They are purchased in packages via the The Treasury.
  • Tokens are used to send appreciation gifts to Chefs and to purchase combat artifacts.
  • Tokens have no cash value and cannot be withdrawn. See Purchases & VAT for full legal terms.

Battle Gifts

Viewers can send a combat artifact directly to a Chef during an active battle. The artifact is delivered instantly and the Chef can use it in the current fight.

  • You choose any artifact from the shop and select the Chef you want to send it to.
  • The cost is the artifact's token price plus a virtual delivery fee, a small surcharge for real-time in-battle delivery.
  • The recipient Chef receives the artifact in their inventory immediately, even mid-combat.
  • Battle Gift artifacts are one-use digital combat items. If used in combat, the artifact is consumed and cannot be used again. A historical record may remain on the Chef profile or battle log.
  • Multiple deliveries of the same artifact to the same Chef are allowed.
  • Battle Gifts are digital items only. No physical goods are supplied.

Artifact Drops

After every completed battle, Chefs receive a random artifact from the arena vault.

  • Winner: guaranteed drop. The winner always receives at least one artifact. If all artifacts of the selected rarity are already owned, the system picks from any remaining rarity.
  • Defeated: 50% chance of a consolation drop. Nothing is guaranteed for the defeated chef.
  • Artifacts cannot be duplicated. A Chef cannot receive an artifact they already own.
  • Artifacts are displayed on the Chef's profile. Artifacts consumed during combat are marked as used.
  • Artifacts granted directly by the Arena Operator are for in-arena use only. They cannot be converted into tokens, redeemed for cash, traded, transferred, or withdrawn from the platform in any form. See Operator-Issued Artifacts in the Purchases & VAT policy.

Combat Artifacts

  • There are 200 combat artifacts: 100 attack and 100 defence, across 5 rarities.
  • Artifacts can be purchased in the The Treasury using tokens.
  • Rarity determines token cost: Common 10T, Uncommon 25T, Rare 60T, Epic 150T, Legendary 400T.
  • Attack artifacts target opponent ingredients. Defence artifacts protect your own.

Closed-Loop Token Model

  • Tokens are a closed-loop digital currency. They can only be spent within the Chef Battles arena on appreciation gifts and combat artifacts.
  • Tokens have no monetary value and cannot be exchanged for cash, transferred to another account, or redeemed for goods or services outside the arena.
  • Token balances do not expire while your account remains active, but CulinEire reserves the right to expire unused balances after 24 months of account inactivity.
  • All token transactions are recorded in an immutable ledger. Once recorded, a transaction cannot be reversed except under the refund policy in Purchases & VAT.
  • Token packages are priced inclusive of applicable VAT. The VAT breakdown is shown at checkout.

CBR & LSR Rewards

CulinEire may issue discretionary rewards to Chefs and live supporters as recognition of contribution to the arena community.

  • Chef Battle Rewards (CBR) are internal reward records granted to Chefs who win battles, reach level milestones, or are recognised for outstanding performance.
  • Live Support Rewards (LSR) are internal reward records issued to viewers who send qualifying appreciation gifts.
  • CBR and LSR are not tokens, not money, and not e-money. They are discretionary platform reward records held internally by CulinEire until approved for payout. They are not guaranteed and do not constitute a contractual entitlement.
  • Each reward record passes through up to 11 lifecycle stages: Pending, Queued, Approved, Issued, Acknowledged, Used, Expired, Reversed, Disputed, Voided, Archived. Only Approved records are eligible for payout.
  • Winning a battle does not automatically create a payout. The Next Battle Unlock condition must be met first (see section 16).
  • If a Stripe Connect payout account is set up and all conditions are met, a Chef may request a payout at a rate of 1 approved reward token = €0.025. Minimum payout: €50 (2,000 tokens). Payouts are subject to admin review and are not automatic.
  • Reward amounts, eligibility criteria and payout rates may change at any time without notice.
  • The following language is never used in connection with CBR/LSR: "Chef funds", "earned money", "withdrawable balance", "guaranteed payout", "money owed", "cash balance".

Next Battle Unlock

  • CBR/LSR reward records from Battle N are not payout-eligible until the Chef completes a subsequent eligible battle (Battle N+1).
  • An eligible battle requires: challenge accepted, recipe submitted, combat completed, cooking phase completed, dish photo uploaded, moderator approved, voting completed. Abandoned, disqualified, cancelled or fraud-flagged battles do not qualify.
  • After completing a battle, both Chefs also enter a 24-hour cooldown before they can issue or accept a new challenge.
  • Chefs who have active challenge requests or pending battles cannot issue additional challenges until those are resolved.
  • The cooldown period may be reduced or extended by CulinEire at season or event boundaries.

Anti-Gambling

  • Chef Battles is a skill-based cooking competition. It is not a gambling product.
  • Tokens are not stakes. Purchasing tokens or sending gifts does not constitute placing a bet and does not entitle the sender to any prize or monetary return.
  • Tokens may not be staked. Battle outcome does not transfer tokens between users. CBR/LSR are only created through approved platform logic.
  • Voting determines the winner based on the quality of submitted recipes, not on token spending or artifact use.
  • Artifact combat is an entertainment mechanic during the cooking phase. It does not directly determine the battle outcome.
  • CulinEire does not operate under a gambling licence and does not offer odds, prize pools, jackpots, or cash winnings.
  • If you are concerned about your spending in the arena, contact us at any time via the contact form.

Tax & DAC7

  • Chefs are solely responsible for their own tax affairs in relation to any CBR/LSR payouts received from CulinEire.
  • CBR/LSR payouts may constitute taxable income under Irish and EU tax law. CulinEire does not provide tax advice.
  • Under DAC7 / EU Mandatory Disclosure rules (MRDP), CulinEire may be required to collect and report certain information about Chefs who receive qualifying payouts to Revenue Commissioners (Ireland) or other competent tax authorities.
  • Before any payout is processed, CulinEire will collect required data including: legal name, date of birth, address, country of tax residence, and tax identification number.
  • If you receive CBR/LSR payouts above applicable reporting thresholds, CulinEire may be legally required to report this to Revenue. You will be informed if this applies to you.
  • CulinEire reserves the right to withhold payouts pending collection of required tax identification information.

  • You must not consume alcohol to excess or use any illegal substances during a stream.
  • You must not make defamatory statements about any person, business, or brand.

Recording and Moderation

Culinary Factions

Every Chef represents two culinary identities and competes for both each season: a Cuisine (heritage — e.g. Italian, Japanese, French) and a Specialty (mastery — e.g. BBQ, Bakery, Molecular).

  • Cuisines and Specialties are a fixed set curated by CulinEire. You choose one of each at enrolment — you do not create your own.
  • Factions are seasonal teams. You may change your Cuisine or Specialty between seasons; while a season is active, your choice is locked.
  • Your approved culinary activity (recipes, articles, Pinches, likes received, and battle results) earns contribution points that count toward both your Cuisine’s and your Specialty’s seasonal standing.
  • Contribution points are skill-based and earned only through approved platform activity. They are not tokens, cannot be bought, boosted, or staked, and battle outcomes never transfer points between Chefs.
  • Faction standings are ranked by contribution normalised for faction size, so a faction cannot win by headcount alone. Battles between two Chefs of the same faction award no faction points, and anti-abuse rules apply.
  • Season rewards are non-cash recognition only. Winning and top-performing factions and their Chefs are honoured with legendary combat artifacts, a place in the Hall of Fame, and permanent season medals that stay on the Chef’s account forever. There is no cash payout for faction or season results.
  • Any monetary reward would only ever arise from an optional external sponsor’s own initiative. It is not a core part of the arena, is not guaranteed, and would be handled separately from these rules.
  • Not gambling. Choosing or representing a Cuisine or Specialty is not a bet or wager. There is no prize pool, jackpot, pot, odds, or cash winnings tied to faction outcomes. Standings reflect skill-based culinary contribution, consistent with the Anti-Gambling section.

Clans

A Clan is a team of Chefs who choose to participate together under a shared name.

Each Clan has one Founder and may include additional members. The Founder creates the Clan, selects its name and chooses up to three categories from the Cuisines and Specialties currently available on the Platform. A Clan cannot select every available category.

The selected categories identify the areas in which the Clan is prepared to participate. They do not replace, restrict or change the personal Cuisine, Specialty or professional identity of any individual member.

The same category may be selected by more than one Clan. Selecting a category does not give a Clan ownership, control or exclusive rights over any Cuisine, Specialty, culinary tradition or Platform category.

Clan Names

Clan names are subject to the Platform Rules and moderation.

A Clan name must not:

  • be unlawful, abusive, threatening, hateful or discriminatory;
  • impersonate another person, Clan, business or organisation;
  • infringe another person's intellectual property or other legal rights;
  • falsely suggest endorsement, sponsorship, approval or an official relationship with CulinEire or another party;
  • deliberately mislead users about the Clan's identity, status or authority; or
  • create a risk to the safety, fairness, integrity or reputation of the Platform.

CulinEire may reject a proposed Clan name or require an existing Clan name to be changed where reasonably necessary to enforce these Rules.

Clan Membership

Each Chef remains individually responsible for their account, submissions, conduct and compliance with the Platform Rules.

Clan membership does not permit one member to:

  • access, operate or share another member's account;
  • submit content under another person's identity;
  • transfer personal scores, Moves, Artifacts, roles or account features;
  • represent another member without permission;
  • create duplicate or false accounts;
  • coordinate artificial voting or activity; or
  • bypass any eligibility, Battle, voting, moderation or security requirement.

Joining a Clan does not create an employment, partnership, agency, ownership or representative relationship between its members or between the Clan and CulinEire.

Clan Categories

A Clan may participate in each of its selected categories where that category is enabled for the relevant season, event or Battle.

Clan categories do not provide a scoring advantage. They do not determine the seasonal winner and do not increase the value of any contribution.

CulinEire may update the list of available Cuisines and Specialties between seasons. Where reasonably necessary, CulinEire may also require a Clan to update its category selection before participating in a new season.

Seasonal Clan Standings

Clan standings are determined by the combined eligible seasonal contributions of the Clan's members.

Only activity that has been completed, approved and accepted under the Platform Rules may count towards a Clan's seasonal contribution.

Activity may be excluded or deducted where it is:

  • rejected or removed;
  • duplicated;
  • fraudulent or misleading;
  • created through false or duplicate accounts;
  • artificially coordinated;
  • produced in breach of the Platform Rules;
  • used to manipulate scores or standings; or
  • otherwise declared ineligible under the applicable seasonal rules.

At the end of the season, the highest-ranked Clan is the Clan whose members have earned the greatest combined eligible seasonal contribution, subject to final verification by CulinEire.

Where two or more Clans have the same verified seasonal contribution, the published tie-break rules for that season will determine their final positions.

Alliances

An Alliance is a group of Clans that choose to cooperate in designated Chef Battle activities.

Each Clan within an Alliance remains a separate team with its own Founder, members, categories, seasonal contribution and position in the Clan standings.

Joining an Alliance does not:

  • merge the participating Clans;
  • transfer control of one Clan to another;
  • combine Clan ownership;
  • allow one Clan to act legally on behalf of another;
  • create an employment, partnership, agency or representative relationship; or
  • give the Alliance ownership or exclusive rights over any Cuisine, Specialty, category or culinary tradition.

Different Alliances may represent or participate in the same categories where permitted by the applicable seasonal rules.

  • bypassing any Battle, voting, eligibility, moderation or security rule.

All Alliance activity remains subject to the Chef Battle Rules, the Code of Conduct and CulinEire's moderation, security and integrity controls.

CulinEire may reject, remove or disregard Alliance activity that breaches these Rules or undermines the fairness or integrity of the Platform.

The competition is between participating teams, dishes and eligible Platform contributions. It is not a competition between peoples or cultures.

Season Champion Recognition

The winning Clan of each season may receive a non-cash form of Platform recognition for the following season: the opportunity to nominate up to two eligible members of that Clan to serve as Arena Observers.

This recognition does not provide a cash prize, Token reward, prize pool, payout or other financial benefit.

Clan Champion

For the purpose of Arena Observer nominations, the Clan Champion is the member of the winning Clan who earned the highest verified individual seasonal contribution.

Only eligible activity approved under the Platform Rules may count towards that contribution.

Where two or more members have the same verified contribution, the published tie-break rules for the relevant season will determine the Clan Champion.

Observer Nominations

The Clan Champion may nominate up to two eligible members of the winning Clan during the nomination window specified by CulinEire.

The Clan Champion may nominate themselves, provided they satisfy the same eligibility requirements as any other nominee.

A nominated member is not required to accept the role.

A nomination becomes effective only after the nominee:

  • accepts the nomination;
  • satisfies the applicable eligibility requirements;
  • agrees to comply with the Arena Observer Rules; and
  • is confirmed by CulinEire.

CulinEire may reject a nomination where the nominee:

  • is suspended, restricted or otherwise ineligible;
  • has committed a serious or repeated breach of the Platform Rules;
  • presents an unresolved conflict of interest;
  • refuses to accept the Observer Rules;
  • creates a safety, privacy, fairness or integrity risk; or
  • is otherwise unsuitable for the limited responsibilities of the role.

If a valid nomination is not completed within the nomination window, the relevant Observer seat will remain vacant.

A vacant seat will not automatically pass to another Chef, Clan or Alliance.

Status of an Arena Observer

An Arena Observer position is personal to the appointed member. It:

  • cannot be sold, assigned, transferred or exchanged;
  • has no monetary or redeemable value;
  • does not entitle the Observer to Tokens, payments, compensation or other financial benefits;
  • does not create employment with CulinEire;
  • does not create an agency, partnership or representative relationship;
  • does not make the Observer an official spokesperson for CulinEire; and
  • does not give the Observer ownership of, or authority over, the Platform.

An Arena Observer is not:

  • a general Platform moderator;
  • a Platform administrator;
  • an employee or legal representative of CulinEire;
  • an arbitrator or independent adjudicator;
  • entitled to determine the result of a dispute;
  • entitled to block a decision made by CulinEire; or
  • entitled to unrestricted access to administrative tools, user accounts or private information.

Role of an Arena Observer

An Arena Observer has a limited advisory role in Chef Battle disputes referred to them by CulinEire.

CulinEire determines:

  • which disputes may be referred to an Observer;
  • which questions the Observer may consider;
  • what information and supporting material may be provided; and
  • the period within which the Observer may submit an opinion.

An Observer may provide an opinion only on the information made available for that purpose.

The Observer's input may be:

  • recorded as part of the dispute record;
  • shown to the Platform operator;
  • considered alongside other available evidence; and
  • taken into account when CulinEire reaches its decision.

The Observer's input is advisory only. It does not:

  • bind CulinEire;
  • bind the participating Chefs;
  • determine the outcome of the dispute;
  • prevent CulinEire from reaching a different conclusion; or
  • create a right of appeal or review.

All final moderation, integrity, disciplinary and dispute decisions remain solely with CulinEire.

Observer Conflicts of Interest

An Arena Observer must act honestly, respectfully and impartially.

An Observer must promptly disclose any actual, potential or perceived conflict of interest.

A conflict may include:

  • a personal relationship with a participant;
  • current or recent membership of the same Clan;
  • membership of an allied or opposing Clan;
  • a financial or commercial connection;
  • involvement in the relevant Battle;
  • a competitive interest in the outcome; or
  • any other connection that could reasonably affect the Observer's impartiality.

CulinEire may require an Observer to take no part in a dispute where a conflict exists or may reasonably be perceived to exist.

CulinEire may refer the matter to another available Observer or decide it without Observer input.

An Observer who fails to disclose a relevant conflict may be suspended or removed from the role.

Observer Conduct and Confidentiality

An Arena Observer must not:

  • disclose non-public information received through the role;
  • use dispute information for personal or competitive advantage;
  • share restricted material with their Clan, Alliance or any other person;
  • contact, pressure, threaten or harass a participant regarding a dispute;
  • request or accept money, Tokens, Gifts, services or any other benefit in exchange for an opinion;
  • coordinate an opinion or outcome with another Observer or participant;
  • misuse personal information;
  • interfere with evidence;
  • make a false or misleading statement about the dispute; or
  • claim to speak or act on behalf of CulinEire.

Information provided to an Observer may be limited, redacted or anonymised where reasonably necessary to protect privacy, security or the integrity of the dispute process.

The Observer must use the information only for the dispute for which it was provided.

Suspension and Removal

CulinEire may suspend or remove an Arena Observer before the end of their appointment where the Observer:

  • breaches the Platform Rules or Arena Observer Rules;
  • misuses the role;
  • fails to disclose a conflict of interest;
  • breaches confidentiality;
  • attempts to influence a dispute improperly;
  • accepts or requests a prohibited benefit;
  • becomes suspended or otherwise ineligible;
  • creates a safety, privacy, fairness or integrity risk; or
  • no longer remains suitable for the role.

Removal from the role does not create a right to compensation, replacement recognition or another Platform benefit.

CulinEire is not required to fill a seat that becomes vacant during the season.

Duration of the Observer Role

An Arena Observer appointment applies only during the season immediately following the winning Clan's victory.

The appointment begins when the nominee is confirmed by CulinEire and expires automatically at the end of that season.

The appointment does not continue into a later season.

A previous Observer may serve again only if they are validly nominated, remain eligible and are confirmed under the Rules applicable to the later season.

The Arena Observer opportunity is a non-cash, non-transferable form of Platform recognition. It is not a prize pool, stake, wager, financial reward or payment for services.
